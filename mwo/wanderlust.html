Wanderlust

Explore the unexplored

Wanderlust was an assignment for Digital Grid Systems class in school. The assignment was to create an identity for a magazine and create a magazine layout for an article surrounding a theme that the magazine was based around. I love travelling and nature. For some time now, I've been in desperate need for a vacation and I'd been feeling what some call wanderlust. It was this desire that inspired the identity.

I created this logo for Wanderlust to represent an urge to explore places exciting and new. I've always been drawn to the idea of a life in nature, and surviving on your own. I chose the symbols to represent exploring and nature, and placed it in a diamond, a shape long associated with the cardinal rose, separating the four icons in the quadrants of the cardinal directions.

I'm also very much drawn to California, its somewhere I hope to live sometime soon. I chose this issue to be about different places in California. The shore image for the cover was a nice addition because its colors played especially nice with the brand's colors. The typography for the cover and the issue is Raleway.

I chose a clean layout that relied on the beauty of the subject to deliver a compelling and enjoyable reading experience. The name of the magazine is Wanderlust, and I believe a heavy focus on the imagery is a great way to encite a desire to travel to those places.

The article was about the 17 Mile Drive, a portion of California's costal Highway 1. I used imagery to represent locations like Monterey Bay, Big Sur, and San Francisco Bay. All images were found on Unsplash, a great stock photography site.
